Our Story

From a small workshop in Mombasa to creating handcrafted wooden kitchenware cherished across Kenya and beyond.

The Demba Legacy

Founded in 1995 by master craftsman James Demba, our journey began in a humble workshop in Mombasa. What started as a passion for traditional woodworking has grown into a family business dedicated to creating beautiful, functional kitchenware that stands the test of time.

For nearly three decades, we've combined age-old techniques with modern design sensibilities. Each piece in our collection tells a story of dedication, craftsmanship, and a deep respect for the natural beauty of wood.

Today, while we've grown, our commitment remains the same: to create sustainable, handcrafted wooden products that bring warmth and character to kitchens across Kenya and beyond.

Our Values

The principles that guide everything we create

Sustainability

We source wood from certified sustainable forests and use eco-friendly finishes. Every piece is crafted with minimal environmental impact.

Craftsmanship

Each piece is handcrafted by skilled artisans using traditional techniques passed down through generations of woodworkers.

Quality

We don't compromise on quality. Every item undergoes rigorous inspection to ensure it meets our high standards of durability and beauty.

Community

We support local communities by providing training and employment opportunities, and sourcing materials from local suppliers.

Our Journey

Key milestones in our story of growth and craftsmanship

1995

The Beginning

James Demba opens a small woodworking shop in Mombasa, focusing on traditional kitchen utensils.

2005

First Workshop

We move to our current location on Kilifi Main Street and establish our first proper workshop with three artisans.

2015

National Recognition

Demba Woodcraft receives the National Artisan Excellence Award for outstanding craftsmanship and quality.

2023

Digital Expansion

Launch of our online store, allowing customers across Kenya and beyond to access our handcrafted products.
